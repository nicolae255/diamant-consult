Selling in Ixelles, Uccle
or Etterbeek.

Buyer profiles, price per m², and what makes each municipality different — how Diamant Consult operates in Brussels' most active markets.

Three municipalities, three distinct markets

Ixelles, Uccle and Etterbeek are often grouped together as Brussels' "premium" communes. But their property markets are actually quite different depending on the neighbourhood, property type and buyer profile. Here's what you need to know before listing.

Ixelles: Brussels' tightest market

Ixelles is the municipality where demand structurally outpaces supply. Well-positioned properties — around the ponds, Rue du Bailli, Avenue de la Toison d'Or, Place Flagey — sell within days with multiple simultaneous offers. The median price exceeds €3,500/m² for apartments and can reach €4,500/m² for well-renovated, well-located houses.

The buyer profile is diverse: expats from the European Quarter, young DINK couples (double income, no kids), buy-to-let investors. Demand for properties with terraces or gardens exploded post-Covid and hasn't subsided.

In Ixelles, the challenge isn't finding buyers — it's correctly managing several simultaneous offers and choosing the best one. That's precisely what we do: structure the viewing window to create healthy competition and maximise the final price.

Uccle: the market for discerning families

Uccle is Brussels' quintessential municipality for families with children. Large houses, gardens, proximity to European and international schools — demand is strong and buyers have purchasing power. The median price ranges from €3,200 to €4,000/m² depending on the neighbourhood.

What differs in Uccle compared to Ixelles: buyers take more time. They visit multiple times, request renovation quotes, consult their architects. The sales process is longer but prices are solid. Patience matters — don't undersell under pressure.

The fastest-selling properties in Uccle: turnkey houses between €700k and €1.2M with a decent EPC. Properties requiring renovation also find buyers, provided the price honestly accounts for the cost of works.

Etterbeek: the value-for-money option that attracts buyers

Etterbeek enjoys an exceptional central position — steps from the Cinquantenaire, the European Quarter and Brussels-Luxembourg station — at prices still below Ixelles. This draws an informed first-time buyer clientele and investors seeking yield.

The median price is around €2,800–3,200/m². Well-located two-bedroom apartments are the most sought-after. Multi-unit investment properties are also highly researched by investors.

In Etterbeek, presentation is decisive: buyers compare a lot and are sensitive to photo quality and overall condition. A well-presented property stands out quickly from the rest of the market.
